**Mission of The Crate Institute of Design**  
  
**Statement of Purpose**  
  
The mission of The Crate Institute of Design is to collect, preserve, document, study, and share knowledge about the designed world.  
  
The Institute exists to deepen understanding of design as a cultural force and to ensure that meaningful artifacts, ideas, systems, and histories remain accessible for future generations.  
  
Its work is guided by curiosity, observation, documentation, and stewardship.  
  
⸻  
  
**Why The Institute Exists**  
  
Design surrounds everyday life.  
  
It shapes how people communicate, navigate, learn, work, play, and understand the world around them.  
  
Yet much of design history remains undocumented, overlooked, or forgotten.  
  
Objects disappear.  
  
Publications fall out of circulation.  
  
Technologies become obsolete.  
  
Knowledge becomes fragmented.  
  
The Institute exists to help prevent that loss.  
  
Its purpose is to preserve evidence of design thinking across time, disciplines, and cultures.  
  
⸻  
  
**Stewardship of Knowledge**  
  
The Institute believes that knowledge carries responsibility.  
  
Information that is not documented is eventually lost.  
  
Research that is not preserved becomes inaccessible.  
  
Artifacts that are not understood become disconnected from their historical context.  
  
For this reason, the Institute regards stewardship as one of its primary obligations.  
  
Stewardship means caring for knowledge in a way that allows future generations to discover, study, and build upon it.  
  
⸻  
  
**Preservation Through Documentation**  
  
The Institute recognizes documentation as a form of preservation.  
  
A photograph can preserve an object.  
  
A field note can preserve an observation.  
  
A publication can preserve a moment in history.  
  
An archive can preserve an entire body of knowledge.  
  
The Institute therefore treats documentation not as an administrative task, but as a cultural responsibility.  
  
Every documented discovery becomes part of a larger collective memory.  
  
⸻  
  
**Encouraging Curiosity**  
  
The Institute believes curiosity is one of the most important drivers of learning.  
  
Many of the most significant discoveries begin not with expertise, but with questions.  
  
Why was this object made?  
  
How was this system developed?  
  
What conditions produced this outcome?  
  
What can be learned from it today?  
  
The Institute seeks to cultivate a culture of inquiry where questions are valued as highly as answers.  
  
⸻  
  
**Learning Across Disciplines**  
  
The Institute rejects the notion that creative disciplines exist in isolation.  
  
Graphic design informs architecture.  
  
Industrial design influences digital products.  
  
Music culture shapes visual culture.  
  
Publishing affects information design.  
  
Skateboarding influences typography.  
  
Technology changes communication.  
  
The Institute studies the connections between disciplines because meaningful insights often emerge at their intersections.  
  
⸻  
  
**Preserving Everyday Design**  
  
The Institute believes cultural value is not limited to celebrated masterpieces.  
  
Meaningful lessons can be found in ordinary objects.  
  
Instruction manuals.  
  
Transit maps.  
  
Album covers.  
  
Product packaging.  
  
Store signage.  
  
Magazine layouts.  
  
Public notices.  
  
Wayfinding systems.  
  
Tools.  
  
Artifacts of everyday life often reveal more about a culture than its monuments.  
  
The Institute therefore places equal value on the ordinary and the exceptional.  
  
⸻  
  
**Building a Living Archive**  
  
The Institute does not seek to create a static collection.  
  
Its goal is to build a living archive.  
  
An archive that continues to grow through observation, research, discovery, and contribution.  
  
The archive is never complete.  
  
Every new object adds context.  
  
Every new document expands understanding.  
  
Every new question reveals additional paths for investigation.  
  
The Institute remains committed to maintaining an archive that evolves alongside the culture it studies.  
  
⸻  
  
**Long-Term Responsibility**  
  
The Institute accepts a long-term responsibility to the preservation of design knowledge.  
  
Its role is not to dictate trends.  
  
Its role is not to predict the future.  
  
Its role is to observe carefully, document thoroughly, preserve responsibly, and share knowledge openly.  
  
By doing so, the Institute hopes to contribute to a broader understanding of how design shapes human experience.  
  
⸻  
  
**Mission Summary**  
  
The mission of The Crate Institute of Design is:  
  
To collect what might be forgotten.  
  
To document what might be overlooked.  
  
To preserve what might disappear.  
  
To study what others pass by.  
  
To share what is discovered.  
  
To ensure that the history, systems, and artifacts of design remain available for future exploration.
