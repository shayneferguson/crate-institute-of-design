**Principles of The Crate Institute of Design**  
  
**Introduction**  
  
The following principles guide the daily work of The Crate Institute of Design.  
  
They are not rules.  
  
They are observations developed through years of collecting, documenting, researching, and studying the designed world.  
  
Together they form a practical philosophy for inquiry.  
  
⸻  
  
**01**  
  
**Follow Curiosity**  
  
Curiosity is the beginning of most discoveries.  
  
Meaningful research often begins with a simple question.  
  
An unfamiliar object.  
  
An unusual detail.  
  
An overlooked artifact.  
  
A pattern that cannot yet be explained.  
  
The Institute encourages the pursuit of questions before answers.  
  
Curiosity provides direction.  
  
Research provides understanding.  
  
⸻  
  
**02**  
  
**Collect Context, Not Just Objects**  
  
An object without context reveals only part of its story.  
  
Whenever possible, researchers should document where an artifact originated, how it was used, who created it, and the circumstances surrounding its existence.  
  
The goal is not simply to preserve objects.  
  
The goal is to preserve understanding.  
  
Context transforms artifacts into evidence.  
  
⸻  
  
**03**  
  
**Observe Before Interpreting**  
  
The Institute values observation before conclusion.  
  
Researchers are encouraged to record what is visible before attempting to explain what it means.  
  
Assumptions can obscure important details.  
  
Careful observation often reveals information that initial interpretations overlook.  
  
The first responsibility is to see clearly.  
  
⸻  
  
**04**  
  
**Document While Discovering**  
  
Documentation should occur alongside research rather than after it.  
  
Observations recorded in the moment often contain details that are later forgotten.  
  
Field notes, photographs, sketches, recordings, and written reflections help preserve the path of discovery.  
  
Documentation captures not only findings but also the process that produced them.  
  
⸻  
  
**05**  
  
**Preserve Before Improving**  
  
Many artifacts contain valuable information precisely because they have not been altered.  
  
Wear, imperfections, annotations, modifications, and signs of use often reveal important aspects of an object’s history.  
  
The Institute prioritizes preservation before restoration.  
  
Evidence should not be erased in the pursuit of perfection.  
  
⸻  
  
**06**  
  
**Value the Ordinary**  
  
Extraordinary objects often attract attention.  
  
Ordinary objects often reveal understanding.  
  
A transit map.  
  
A user manual.  
  
A supermarket flyer.  
  
A product label.  
  
A record sleeve.  
  
A notebook.  
  
Everyday artifacts provide direct evidence of how design operates within daily life.  
  
The Institute studies the ordinary because culture is largely built from ordinary things.  
  
⸻  
  
**07**  
  
**Study Relationships**  
  
Objects rarely exist in isolation.  
  
Every artifact is connected to technologies, industries, cultures, materials, environments, and people.  
  
The Institute seeks to understand these relationships.  
  
Research should extend beyond the object itself to include the systems surrounding it.  
  
Connections often reveal more than individual artifacts.  
  
⸻  
  
**08**  
  
**Follow the Trail**  
  
One discovery often leads to another.  
  
A designer references a publication.  
  
A publication references a movement.  
  
A movement references a technology.  
  
A technology references a social condition.  
  
Research should remain open to unexpected paths.  
  
The Institute recognizes that valuable discoveries are often found while pursuing something else.  
  
⸻  
  
**09**  
  
**Leave Evidence Behind**  
  
Future researchers should be able to understand how conclusions were reached.  
  
Sources, observations, references, and methodologies should be documented whenever possible.  
  
Knowledge becomes more valuable when others can trace its origins.  
  
The Institute values transparency in research.  
  
A documented path benefits everyone who follows it.  
  
⸻  
  
**10**  
  
**Remain Open to Revision**  
  
New information can change existing understanding.  
  
New evidence can reveal overlooked perspectives.  
  
Research should remain flexible enough to accommodate new discoveries.  
  
The Institute treats knowledge as evolving rather than fixed.  
  
Certainty should never prevent learning.  
  
⸻  
  
**11**  
  
**Think Across Disciplines**  
  
Design does not exist within isolated categories.  
  
Graphic design influences architecture.  
  
Music culture influences visual culture.  
  
Industrial design affects digital products.  
  
Technology reshapes communication.  
  
Researchers are encouraged to look beyond disciplinary boundaries and explore the intersections between fields.  
  
Important insights often emerge where categories overlap.  
  
⸻  
  
**12**  
  
**The Archive Is Never Finished**  
  
No collection is complete.  
  
No archive contains everything.  
  
No investigation answers every question.  
  
The Institute views incompleteness not as a weakness but as an invitation.  
  
There is always more to discover.  
  
More to document.  
  
More to understand.  
  
The work continues.  
  
⸻  
  
**Closing Statement**  
  
The purpose of these principles is not to prescribe a single method of inquiry.  
  
Their purpose is to encourage attentiveness.  
  
To cultivate curiosity.  
  
To support documentation.  
  
To preserve understanding.  
  
And to ensure that the study of design remains an active and ongoing exploration of the world around us.  
  
Researchers of the Institute are encouraged to carry these principles into every collection, observation, publication, and investigation they undertake.
