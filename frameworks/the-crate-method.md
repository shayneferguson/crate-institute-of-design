# The Crate Method™

> A research-driven framework for understanding, clarifying, and communicating identity. Six phases from observation to longevity.

---

## Overview

The Crate Method™ is the proprietary process through which The Crate Institute of Design approaches every engagement. It ensures that design decisions are grounded in research, not assumption.

See also: [[identity-excavation-framework]], [[signal-extraction-method]], [[connected-systems-framework]]

---

## Phase 01 — Observe

**Activities:** Research. Interviews. Artifacts. History. Context.

**Goal:** Understanding.

The first phase is purely investigative. No recommendations, no design directions — only listening and gathering.

---

## Phase 02 — Interpret

**Activities:** Identify patterns. Separate signals from noise. Reveal what matters.

**Goal:** Insight.

Raw research is translated into meaning. What did we actually learn? What patterns repeat? What surprises emerged?

---

## Phase 03 — Clarify

**Activities:** Define identity, positioning, message, and purpose.

**Goal:** Alignment.

Strategic clarity before any creative work begins. All stakeholders aligned on who the organization is and what it stands for.

---

## Phase 04 — Systemize

**Activities:** Build visual systems, language systems, digital systems.

**Goal:** Cohesion.

The strategic clarity from Phase 03 is translated into designed systems that work together.

---

## Phase 05 — Implement

**Activities:** Deploy systems consistently across every touchpoint.

**Goal:** Communication.

Systems are only valuable in use. Implementation ensures the work lands in the world correctly.

---

## Phase 06 — Evolve

**Activities:** Maintain coherence while adapting to change.

**Goal:** Longevity.

Identity is not static. This phase ensures the system can grow with the organization without losing coherence.
