# Tone & Language

> How The Crate Institute of Design sounds — in proposals, on social, in emails, and everywhere else.

---

## Voice Attributes

- **Declarative, not hedging** — short, direct sentences that state a perspective clearly. No "we feel that" or "we believe that perhaps."
- **Thoughtful, not academic** — depth without jargon. We explain complex ideas simply.
- **Confident, not arrogant** — we hold a clear point of view on design without dismissing clients or other approaches.
- **Warm, not casual** — human presence in the writing, but professional gravity.
- **Timeless, not trendy** — language that doesn't chase the moment. We avoid buzzwords.

## Structural Patterns (from foundation document)

The source document reveals a distinctive voice pattern worth preserving:

- **Short, punchy declaratives followed by expansion.** ("Most businesses do not have a design problem. They have a clarity problem.")
- **Parallel construction.** Lists of three or four short phrases that build rhythm. ("Before creating solutions, we investigate. Before designing, we listen. Before making assumptions, we research.")
- **Plain language for deep ideas.** The ideas are sophisticated; the sentences are simple.
- **Named tension.** We often name the gap ("Their work has evolved. Their brand has not.") before offering the resolution.

## Vocabulary

### Words / Phrases We Use
- Clarity, coherence, confidence, purpose
- Uncover, reveal, discover, translate
- Thoughtful, intentional, meaningful, timeless
- Research, strategy, exploration, synthesis
- Patterns, stories, strengths, systems

### Words We Avoid
- "Modern," "clean," "sleek" (generic design speak)
- "Disrupt," "innovative," "cutting-edge" (overused, trend-driven)
- "Journey," "authentic" (overused in brand/marketing)
- Passive voice and hedging language

## Writing Principles

1. Lead with the truth, not the solution. Name the real problem first.
2. Short sentences create clarity. Long sentences create confusion.
3. Specificity beats generality. "Their website no longer represents them" beats "they have brand challenges."
4. The goal of every sentence is to make the next one necessary.

## Copy Examples (from source)

**On what we believe:**
> "Most businesses do not have a design problem. They have a clarity problem."

**On what design is:**
> "Design is not the final layer. Design is the connective tissue."

**On our approach:**
> "We believe great design begins with curiosity."

**On the mission:**
> "The goal is not simply to make things look better. The goal is to make them make sense."
